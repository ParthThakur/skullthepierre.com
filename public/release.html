In the Quarantine EP, I'm back to my roots. Back in my cozy room in Stockholm where I would lock myself in and write raps to jazzy, old-school hip hop beats. Back then I would sample hidden gems on youtube to make my beats, and I would write songs about my life, experiences, and emotions. I've grown out of sampling, but the themes for the songs always remain the same, life and feeling.

The Quarantine EP was recorded in The Lotts, Meath where I spent fourteen days in isolation. My "studio" was an annex, and I sat by the round little kitchen table and recorded all the songs with the mic in hand. Those were fantastic two weeks, full of pondering, conversations with sheep, and lots of music-making. I'm very much an extrovert, but I'm also no stranger to being by myself in a room all day, and so the quarantine lifestyle suited me well.

As I grow, the music evolves, and at the moment I find myself making very different music than what I used to make. Releasing the Quarantine EP is thereby my way of saying farewell to the old-school, hip-hop producer and rapper I used to be, for a little while. IT'S HIP HOP BABY
